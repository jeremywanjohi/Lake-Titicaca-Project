Community & Family

Shared Stewardship on the Reeds

The reed-built islands function as tight-knit micro-communities. Each household unit generally includes extended family, with elder members guiding decisions on island upkeep and resource sharing. Leadership rotates among respected elders, ensuring collective stewardship.

Livelihoods on Water

Fishing, Handicrafts & Tourism

Fishing is the communal heartbeat. Women specialize in totora-reed handicrafts, crafting boats, mats, and intricate figurines sold to visitors. Community-run tourism now funds education and healthcare, with elders managing visitor flow to protect cultural integrity.
