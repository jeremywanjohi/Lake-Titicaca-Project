Uros Governance System

Consensus, tradition, and continuity on the floating islands

Floating Councils & Consensus

The Uros governance model is built on collective participation. Weekly meetings take place on a shared reed platform where elders, community leaders, and spiritual guides gather to discuss crucial matters like fishing limits, tourism flow, education, and reed harvesting cycles.

Decisions are made through consensus, promoting harmony and shared responsibility. Each voice is respected, and youth are invited to participate, ensuring the survival of both tradition and innovation.

The structure mirrors both necessity and culture—every element from the platform’s construction to the ceremonial language spoken during decisions ties back to the Uros' deep bond with Lake Titicaca.

Weekly Councils

Decisions on education, quotas, and trade are made on floating reed platforms. These sessions involve elders and community representatives in structured discussions.

Youth Participation

Young Uros are encouraged to observe and contribute to governance. This nurtures leadership and ensures the continuity of cultural practices among future generations.

Shared Responsibility

All community members, from youth to elders, share in leadership duties. This model ensures fairness, balance, and resilience in the floating communities’ decision-making.

Decision Ceremonies

Important governance decisions are accompanied by spiritual rituals. These honor ancestors and reinforce the Uros' harmony with nature and their traditions.
