At dusk families unroll shared reed mats and gather by the soft glow of lanterns or solar lamps. Inter-Island Councils

Delegates from neighbouring reed communities paddle out to floating council gatherings to coordinate shared fishing zones, organise large-scale reed replenishment and arrange marriage alliances. Decisions are reached by consensus, weaving a lake-wide social fabric that keeps harmony and resources in healthy balance.
